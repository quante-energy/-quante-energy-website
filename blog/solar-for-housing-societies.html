Understanding RESCO and Zero Investment Model

1. What is RESCO Model?

RESCO (Renewable Energy Service Company) model is a zero upfront investment solar model. In this structure, the solar developer installs, owns, and maintains the rooftop solar plant, while the society pays only for the electricity generated at a pre-agreed tariff.

2. How RESCO Works?

1. Installation

The solar developer installs the rooftop solar plant at no cost to the society.

2. Ownership

The developer retains ownership of the solar system during the contract period (typically 15–25 years).

3. Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)

The society signs a long-term agreement and pays only for electricity consumed at a lower tariff than grid rates.

4. Maintenance

All operations, maintenance, and performance risks are handled by the developer.

Benefits of RESCO Model

  • No upfront capital investment
  • Lower electricity tariff
  • No maintenance responsibility
  • Long-term energy cost savings
  • Reduced carbon footprint

What is Net Metering?

Net metering is a billing mechanism that allows solar system owners to export excess electricity generated from rooftop solar panels to the grid. In return, they receive credits that reduce their electricity bill.

How Net Metering Works?

1. Solar Generates Power

During the day, rooftop solar panels generate electricity from sunlight.

2. Power is Used Internally

The electricity generated is first used to power common area loads such as lifts, pumps, and lighting.

3. Excess Power Exported

If more electricity is generated than consumed, the surplus power is exported to the grid.

4. Bill Adjustment

The electricity distribution company adjusts exported units against imported units in the monthly bill.

Benefits of Net Metering

  • Reduces overall electricity bills
  • Maximizes utilization of solar generation
  • Shorter payback period for solar systems
  • Encourages renewable energy adoption
  • Supports India’s clean energy goals

Net Metering for Housing Societies

In residential societies, net metering significantly reduces common area electricity expenses. Any surplus solar energy generated during low usage hours is credited and adjusted against electricity consumed at night or during cloudy days.
